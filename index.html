I am a Master’s student at the Barcelona School of Economics and a Research Associate at the Grattan Institute. My earlier academic work focused on quantum information and photovoltaic materials, before shifting to economic questions in public policy. As an economic policy researcher, I have contributed to projects measuring inequalities in access to specialist healthcare and modelling household energy consumption and carbon pricing impacts. My current research interests include climate and energy economics, and agent-based modelling approaches to understanding macroeconomic dynamics, housing, and inequality.
